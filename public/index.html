🌲 Indian Forest and Wildlife Management Website

India is one of the world’s most biologically diverse countries, hosting nearly 8% of global biodiversity. Indian forests range from tropical rainforests and mangroves to alpine forests and dry deciduous ecosystems.

These forests support millions of livelihoods, regulate climate, protect watersheds, and provide habitat for rare and endangered species such as the Bengal Tiger, Asiatic Lion, Indian Elephant, One-horned Rhinoceros, and Snow Leopard.

🦜 Biodiversity Highlights

Faunal Diversity

India is home to over 90,000 animal species including mammals, reptiles, birds, amphibians, and insects.

Forest Ecosystems

Tropical evergreen, deciduous, mangrove, montane, and alpine forests form India's ecological backbone.

Conservation Efforts

Scientific monitoring, animal census, protected areas and conservation success.
