Biosphere Reserves of India

Protected areas for biodiversity conservation and sustainable development

Introduction

Biosphere Reserves are large protected areas established to conserve ecosystems, plant and animal species, and genetic diversity, while promoting sustainable use of natural resources.

In India, Biosphere Reserves are designated under the Man and the Biosphere Programme (MAB) of UNESCO and are managed by the Ministry of Environment, Forest and Climate Change.

India currently has 18 Biosphere Reserves, representing diverse ecological regions including mountains, forests, wetlands, deserts, islands, and coastal ecosystems.

Objectives of Biosphere Reserves

  • Conservation of biodiversity and ecosystems
  • Protection of endangered species and habitats
  • Promotion of sustainable development
  • Support for research, education, and monitoring
  • Integration of local communities in conservation

Major Biosphere Reserves of India

Nilgiri Biosphere Reserve (Tamil Nadu, Kerala, Karnataka)

Established: 1986 (India’s first Biosphere Reserve)

The Nilgiri Biosphere Reserve is part of the Western Ghats and is one of the most biologically rich regions in India. It supports evergreen, deciduous forests, grasslands, and diverse wildlife.

Sundarbans Biosphere Reserve (West Bengal)

UNESCO World Heritage Site

The Sundarbans Biosphere Reserve is the world’s largest mangrove ecosystem and is known for its unique tidal forests and mangrove-adapted Royal Bengal Tigers.

Nanda Devi Biosphere Reserve (Uttarakhand)

Located in the Himalayan region, the Nanda Devi Biosphere Reserve includes snow-clad peaks, alpine meadows, and glaciers, supporting rare Himalayan flora and fauna.

Gulf of Mannar Biosphere Reserve (Tamil Nadu)

The Gulf of Mannar is India’s first marine biosphere reserve and contains coral reefs, mangroves, seagrass beds, and rich marine life.

Similipal Biosphere Reserve (Odisha)

Similipal is known for its dense forests, waterfalls, and significant populations of tigers and elephants. It is also a major tribal region.

Great Nicobar Biosphere Reserve (Andaman & Nicobar Islands)

The Great Nicobar Biosphere Reserve protects tropical rainforests, mangroves, coral reefs, and endemic island species.

Zonation in Biosphere Reserves

  • Core Zone: Strictly protected area with no human activity
  • Buffer Zone: Limited activities like research and education
  • Transition Zone: Sustainable human activities permitted
