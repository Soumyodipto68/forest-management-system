National Parks of India

Comprehensive information on India’s protected wildlife areas

Introduction

National Parks in India are legally protected areas established under the Wildlife Protection Act, 1972. These parks aim to conserve wildlife, preserve natural habitats, and protect ecological balance.

India is one of the world’s most biologically diverse countries and is home to over 100 National Parks, spread across forests, grasslands, wetlands, deserts, coastal and marine ecosystems.

Objectives of National Parks

  • Protection of endangered flora and fauna
  • Preservation of natural ecosystems
  • Scientific research and biodiversity monitoring
  • Environmental education and awareness
  • Promotion of responsible eco-tourism

Major National Parks of India

Jim Corbett National Park – Uttarakhand

Established: 1936 (India’s first National Park)

Famous For: Bengal Tiger, Asian Elephant

Jim Corbett National Park is located in the Himalayan foothills and is India’s oldest national park. It was the first park to be included under Project Tiger and is known for its riverine forests, grasslands, and diverse wildlife.

Kaziranga National Park – Assam

UNESCO World Heritage Site

Famous For: One-horned Rhinoceros

Kaziranga National Park is a globally recognized conservation area protecting the largest population of one-horned rhinoceroses. The park also supports tigers, elephants, wild buffalo, and swamp deer.

Ranthambore National Park – Rajasthan

Famous For: Tiger sightings & historic fort

Ranthambore National Park is one of the most popular wildlife tourism destinations in India. The park’s dry deciduous forest and lakes provide excellent visibility for wildlife viewing.

Kanha National Park – Madhya Pradesh

Famous For: Barasingha conservation

Kanha National Park is among the best-managed parks in India and inspired Rudyard Kipling’s “The Jungle Book”. It is known for its successful conservation programs.

Sundarbans National Park – West Bengal

Famous For: Mangrove forests & Royal Bengal Tiger

Sundarbans is the world’s largest mangrove forest ecosystem and supports unique wildlife adapted to saline tidal conditions.

Gir National Park – Gujarat

Famous For: Asiatic Lion

Gir National Park is the only natural habitat of the Asiatic Lion and represents one of the most successful wildlife conservation stories in India.

Bandhavgarh National Park – Madhya Pradesh

Famous For: High tiger density

Bandhavgarh National Park has one of the highest densities of Bengal tigers and rich biodiversity, along with ancient caves and fort ruins.

Best Time to Visit National Parks

  • October – February: Pleasant weather, best for tourism
  • March – June: High wildlife sightings near water sources
  • July – September: Most parks closed due to monsoon

Complete List of National Parks (State-wise)

India has National Parks in almost every state and union territory. Below is a summarized state-wise representation.

  • Madhya Pradesh: Kanha, Bandhavgarh, Pench, Panna, Satpura
  • Assam: Kaziranga, Manas, Orang, Nameri
  • Rajasthan: Ranthambore, Sariska, Desert NP
  • Uttarakhand: Jim Corbett, Rajaji, Valley of Flowers
  • West Bengal: Sundarbans, Gorumara, Jaldapara, Buxa
  • Gujarat: Gir, Blackbuck (Velavadar), Marine NP
  • Karnataka: Bandipur, Nagarhole, Bannerghatta
  • Kerala: Periyar, Silent Valley, Eravikulam
  • Tamil Nadu: Mudumalai, Guindy, Gulf of Mannar
  • Himachal Pradesh: Great Himalayan NP, Pin Valley
  • Ladakh: Hemis National Park
  • Andaman & Nicobar: Mahatma Gandhi Marine NP

Role of National Parks in Conservation

  • Protection of endangered species
  • Habitat preservation
  • Climate change mitigation
  • Scientific research
  • Eco-tourism & livelihood support
