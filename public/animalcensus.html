Animal Census of India

Scientific assessment of wildlife populations across Indian forests

Wildlife census is a systematic process used to estimate the population, distribution, and density of wild animals across different ecosystems. In India, animal census is conducted periodically by the Forest Departments with support from scientific institutions.

Census data plays a crucial role in conservation planning, habitat management, policy formulation, and mitigating human-wildlife conflict.

Census Methodology

  • Direct sighting and block count methods
  • Camera trap surveys
  • Indirect signs (pugmarks, dung, calls)
  • Scientific sampling and GIS mapping
  • Community and forest staff participation

Importance of Animal Census

  • Tracks population trends of endangered species
  • Supports wildlife conservation programs
  • Helps control poaching and illegal trade
  • Guides sustainable safari tourism
  • Assists climate-change impact assessment
